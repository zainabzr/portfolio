Data Analysis

Terrorism and Regime Types: an empirical Analysis

This paper aimed to empirically analyze the relationship between terrorism and regime types, including the various other elements that separate states from each other, such as judicial independence, etc. To evaluate this relationship, I conducted a panel regression analysis of secondary data from the Global Terrorism Database for 13 countries over the time period 2006 to 2018 using Stata. This paper supported existing research that terrorism is significantly impacted by the type of regime, where the weak democracies and failed states suffer more terrorism than other regime types.

Major Research Question: Are regime types and terrorism significantly correlated?
Using: Stata
Includes: Descriptive Statistics, Correlation Matrices, Graphical Representation, Statistical Modeling (Fixed and Random Effects), Regression Analyses, Insights and Conclusions

Exploring Gender and Nationality Narratives in the Halls of MoMA

In 2019, one of the most famous art museums in the world, the Museum of Modern Art, shook the art world with the announcement that it would remain closed for four months to make sweeping changes that align with its efforts towards inclusivity and diversity. This project aims to examine trends in the acquisition of art by the MoMA through the lens of Gender and Nationality.

Research Questions:
  • What is the gender distribution of artists in the MOMA collection?
  • What is the nationality distribution of artists in the MOMA collection?
  • Are there any significant shifts in the acquisition of art by artists with diverse backgrounds?
    • Using: Python (Pandas, Matplotlib, Seaborn, Squarify, and Plotly)
    Includes: Graphical Representation, Descriptive Statistics, Insights and Conclusions
